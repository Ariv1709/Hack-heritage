Learn About The States

Article 152: Defines the term "State" as used in this part of the Constitution.

Article 153: Every state must have a Governor.

Article 154: The executive power of the state is vested in the Governor.

Article 155: The Governor is appointed by the President.

Article 156: The Governor holds office during the pleasure of the President.

Article 157: Specifies the qualifications for appointment as Governor.

Article 158: Specifies the conditions of the Governor's office.

Article 159: The Governor must take an oath of office before assuming duties.

Article 160: The President can make provisions for the discharge of the Governor's functions in certain contingencies.

Article 161: The Governor has the power to grant pardons and other legal remedies in cases within the state's jurisdiction.

Article 162: The executive power of the state extends to matters within the state legislature's jurisdiction.

Article 163: The Governor will act according to the aid and advice of the Council of Ministers, except in certain matters.

Article 164: The Chief Minister and Council of Ministers are appointed by the Governor and hold office during his pleasure.

Article 165: There must be an Advocate General for the state to provide legal advice to the state government.

Article 166: All executive actions of the state must be expressed in the name of the Governor.

Article 167: The Chief Minister has certain duties, including furnishing information to the Governor.

Article 168: Each state will have a legislature, which may consist of two Houses (Legislative Assembly and Legislative Council).

Article 169: Parliament may create or abolish the Legislative Council of a state.

Article 170: Specifies the composition of the Legislative Assemblies.

Article 171: Specifies the composition of the Legislative Councils.

Article 172: Specifies the duration of the Legislative Assemblies and Legislative Councils.

Article 173: Specifies the qualifications for membership in the state legislature.

Article 174: The Governor has the power to summon, prorogue, and dissolve the Legislative Assembly.

Article 175: The Governor can address the state legislature and send messages.

Article 176: The Governor must address the state legislature at the commencement of the first session after each general election.

Article 177: Ministers and the Advocate General have the right to speak in the legislature.

Article 178: There must be a Speaker and Deputy Speaker for the Legislative Assembly.

Article 179: Specifies the process for removal and resignation of the Speaker and Deputy Speaker.

Article 180: In case of a vacancy, the Deputy Speaker performs the duties of the Speaker.

Article 181: The Speaker or Deputy Speaker cannot preside over the house while a resolution for their removal is under consideration.

Article 182: The Legislative Council must have a Chairman and Deputy Chairman.

Article 183: Specifies the process for removal and resignation of the Chairman and Deputy Chairman.

Article 184: In case of a vacancy, the Deputy Chairman performs the duties of the Chairman.

Article 185: The Chairman or Deputy Chairman cannot preside over the house while a resolution for their removal is under consideration.

Article 186: Specifies the salaries and allowances of the Speaker, Deputy Speaker, Chairman, and Deputy Chairman.

Article 187: There must be a secretarial staff for the legislature, and their salaries are charged on the state's Consolidated Fund.

Article 188: Members of the legislature must take an oath or affirmation before taking their seats.

Article 189: Specifies the rules for voting in the legislature.

Article 190: Specifies the conditions for a member's resignation or disqualification.

Article 191: Specifies the disqualifications for membership in the legislature.

Article 192: The Governor will decide questions about disqualification of members.

Article 193: Penalty for sitting and voting while not qualified or disqualified.

Article 194: Specifies the powers, privileges, and immunities of the state legislature and its members.

Article 195: Specifies the salaries and allowances of members of the legislature.

Article 196: Bills can be introduced in either house of the legislature.

Article 197: Restricts the introduction and passing of money bills.

Article 198: Specifies the procedure for passing money bills.

Article 199: Defines money bills.

Article 200: The Governor can give assent to or withhold assent from bills, or reserve them for the President's consideration.

Article 201: When a bill is reserved for the President's consideration, the President can give assent, withhold assent, or return it for reconsideration.

Article 202: The Governor must present the annual financial statement (budget) before the legislature.

Article 203: Specifies the procedure in the legislature with respect to the state's financial matters.

Article 204: Specifies restrictions on expenditure without legislative authorization.

Article 205: The Governor can present supplementary, additional, or excess grants to the legislature.

Article 206: Specifies the procedure for making demands for grants.

Article 207: Specifies the procedure for passing appropriation bills.

Article 208: The legislature must make rules for regulating its procedures and conduct of business.

Article 209: The legislature can regulate its business by making rules that are consistent with the Constitution.

Article 210: The business of the legislature must be conducted in the state's official language or in Hindi/English.

Article 211: No discussion can take place in the legislature regarding the conduct of a judge of the Supreme Court or High Court.

Article 212: The validity of legislative proceedings cannot be questioned in any court.

Article 213: The Governor can promulgate ordinances when the state legislature is not in session.

Article 214: There must be a High Court for each state.

Article 215: Every High Court is a court of record and has the power to punish for contempt of itself.

Article 216: The High Court consists of a Chief Justice and other judges appointed by the President.

Article 217: Specifies the appointment, qualifications, and conditions of service for High Court judges.

Article 218: The provisions related to the removal of a judge of the Supreme Court also apply to High Court judges.

Article 219: Every High Court judge must take an oath or affirmation before assuming office.

Article 220: After retirement, a High Court judge cannot plead or act in any court or authority in India except the Supreme Court or another High Court.

Article 221: Specifies the salaries and allowances of High Court judges.

Article 222: The President can transfer a High Court judge from one High Court to another.

Article 223: The President can appoint a judge of a High Court to discharge the duties of the Chief Justice when the office is vacant.

Article 224: The President can appoint additional judges to the High Court for a temporary period.

Article 224A: The Chief Justice of a High Court, with the President's approval, can request retired High Court judges to act as judges for a temporary period.

Article 225: The jurisdiction of the High Courts is maintained as before the Constitution came into force, subject to the Constitution and laws passed by the legislature.

Article 226: The High Courts have the power to issue writs for enforcement of fundamental rights and other purposes.

Article 227: The High Courts have supervisory jurisdiction over all courts and tribunals within their jurisdiction.

Article 228: The High Court can transfer certain cases from subordinate courts to itself if it involves a substantial question of law.

Article 229: The Chief Justice of the High Court is responsible for the appointment of officers and servants of the court.

Article 230: Parliament can extend the jurisdiction of a High Court to Union territories.

Article 231: Parliament can establish a common High Court for two or more states or Union territories.

Article 233: Specifies the appointment of district judges by the Governor in consultation with the High Court.

Article 234: Appointments of persons other than district judges to the judicial service of the state are made by the Governor.

Article 235: The High Court has control over the subordinate courts.

Article 236: Defines the term "district judge" and specifies the judicial service for a state.

Article 237: The Governor can apply the provisions of this chapter to any class of magistrates in the state.
